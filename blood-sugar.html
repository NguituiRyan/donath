Blood Sugar 101

Insulin & steady energy

Insulin is the hormone that moves sugar from your blood into your cells. When meals spike blood sugar over and over, the body struggles to keep up — leading to crashes, cravings, weight gain and, over time, type-2 diabetes.

The good news: small changes to how and what you eat keep blood sugar steady — which is especially important here, where diabetes is rising fast.

Please note

This is general education, not medical advice. If you have diabetes or take insulin/medication, work with your doctor and Camila before changing your diet.

1

Build every meal around protein & fibre

Beans, ndengu, eggs, fish and lots of greens slow down how fast sugar enters the blood. A plate that's half veg, a quarter protein and a quarter carbs keeps energy steady for hours.

2

Eat your carbs last

Start with vegetables and protein, then eat the ugali or rice. This simple order can noticeably lower the blood-sugar spike from the same meal.

3

Choose slower carbs

Whole-grain or millet ugali, sweet potato, arrowroot and green bananas raise blood sugar more gently than white bread, white rice, mandazi and chapati.

4

Cut liquid sugar

Sodas, sweetened juice and very sugary chai hit the bloodstream fastest. Reducing these is often the single highest-impact change for blood sugar and weight.

5

Walk after meals

A 10–15 minute walk after eating helps muscles soak up glucose, lowering the post-meal spike. It's the easiest "exercise" with a real metabolic payoff. See movement tips →
