Move More

Exercise that fits your life

You don't need a fancy gym to get strong and healthy. Pick the lane that suits you today — and combine cardio with strength for the best results.

Cardio & walking

The most underrated tool for fat loss and blood sugar. Free, joint-friendly, and easy to build into a busy day.

  • Aim for 7,000–10,000 steps a day
  • 10–15 min walk after meals
  • Walk/jog 20–30 min, 3–5×/week
  • Use stairs & walk short trips instead of boda/matatu
  • Progress: add speed or small hills over time

Gym & strength

Lifting weights builds muscle, which raises your TDEE and improves insulin sensitivity. Great 2–4×/week.

  • Focus on big lifts: squat, hinge, push, pull
  • 2–4 sessions per week, full-body or split
  • 3–4 sets of 8–12 reps, leave 1–2 reps in reserve
  • Add a little weight or a rep each week
  • Eat enough protein (see your macro target)

Home workouts

No equipment, no excuses. Bodyweight training builds real strength in your living room.

  • Squats, push-ups (or on knees), lunges
  • Glute bridges, planks, chair dips
  • Circuit: 40s work / 20s rest × 4–6 moves
  • Use water bottles or a bag of maize as weights
  • 3 rounds, 3–4×/week — 20–25 minutes

Warm up, then cool down

Five minutes at the start and end reduces injuries and helps you recover. Don't skip these.

5-minute warm-up

  • March on the spot or skip rope for 2 min
  • Arm, hip and ankle circles
  • 10 bodyweight squats, 10 lunges
  • 5–10 slow push-ups

Cool-down & stretch

  • Walk slowly until your breath settles
  • Stretch thighs, calves and shoulders — hold 20–30s
  • Breathe deeply; never stretch into pain
  • Hydrate and eat protein within the hour

Train smart

1

Add a little each week

Muscles grow when you challenge them slightly more than last time — add a rep, a set or a little weight. This is the single biggest driver of results.

2

Rest and sleep

Your body rebuilds during rest, not during the workout. Aim for 7–9 hours of sleep and take 1–2 rest days a week.

3

Eat enough protein

Protein builds and repairs muscle. Include beans, ndengu, eggs, fish or meat at every meal. Use the TDEE calculator to see your target.

4

Consistency beats perfection

Three short sessions a week for months beats a perfect plan you quit after two weeks. Start small and build.

Avoid these common mistakes

Skipping the warm-upRaises injury risk — just 5 minutes helps.
Doing too much, too soonLeads to burnout and soreness. Build up gradually.
Only doing cardioAdd strength to build muscle and raise your TDEE.
Ignoring foodYou can't out-train a poor diet. They work together.
Poor formStart light; good form before heavy weight.
