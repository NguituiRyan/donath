Eat Local

Nutrition with East-African food

You don't need imported "superfoods." The market down the road has everything you need. Here's how to make the most of what's local, affordable, and in season in Kenya & Tanzania.

Leafy greens & veg

Sukuma wiki, mchicha (amaranth), terere, kunde leaves and cabbage are rich in fibre, iron and folate. Aim to fill half your plate. Lightly cook with minimal oil to keep nutrients.

Affordable protein

Beans (maharagwe), green grams (ndengu), eggs, omena/dagaa, tilapia and chicken. Plant proteins are cheap, filling and great for blood sugar. Include a protein at every meal.

Smarter carbs

Swap white ugali for whole-maize or millet/sorghum ugali. Choose sweet potato (viazi vitamu), arrowroot (nduma), green bananas (matoke) and brown rice for steadier energy.

Healthy fats

Avocado (parachichi), groundnuts (njugu), sesame (ufuta), and coconut in moderation. Use these instead of heavy fried foods to support hormones and keep you full.

Hydration & drinks

Water first. Be careful with sodas, sweetened juice and heavily sugared chai — these spike blood sugar fast. Try unsweetened chai, dawa with less honey, or fruit-infused water.

Eat with the season

Mangoes, oranges, pawpaw, passion and tree tomato when in season are cheaper and tastier. Buy fresh produce at the market in small, frequent amounts to reduce waste.
